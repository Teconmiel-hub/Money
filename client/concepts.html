Compound Interest
Compound interest is interest calculated on the initial principal and accumulated interest from previous periods. It's one of the most powerful concepts in finance, allowing your money to grow exponentially over time.
Investing
Emergency Fund
An emergency fund is money set aside to cover unexpected expenses or financial emergencies. Financial experts recommend saving 3-6 months of living expenses in an easily accessible account.
Savings
Diversification
Diversification is the practice of spreading investments across various financial instruments, industries, and categories to reduce risk. The principle is: don't put all your eggs in one basket.
Investing
Credit Score
A credit score is a numerical expression of your creditworthiness based on your credit history. It ranges from 300-850, with higher scores indicating better credit health and access to better loan terms.
Credit
Budget
A budget is a financial plan that helps you track income and expenses. The 50/30/20 rule suggests allocating 50% to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings and debt repayment.
Budgeting
Inflation
Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises, eroding purchasing power. Understanding inflation is crucial for making smart long-term financial decisions.
